SPRING SONG.
A BLUE-BELL springs the ledge,
upon
A lark sits singing in the hedge ;
Sweet perfumes scent the balmy air,
And life is brimming everywhere.
What lark and breeze and bluebird sing,
Is Spring, Spring, Spring !
No the air is sharp and cold
more ;
The planter wends the wold,
across
And, glad, beneath the shining sky
We wander forth, love and I.
my
And in hearts doth ring
ever our
This of Spring, Spring
song !
For life is life and love is love,
'Twixt maid and dove and dove.
man or
Life be short, life be long,
may may
But love will and
come, to its song
Shall this refrain for cling
ever
Of Spring, Spring, Spring !
