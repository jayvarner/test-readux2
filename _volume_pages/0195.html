Time mocks with
me fair promises ;
A blooming future barren
grows a past,
Like rain fair full-blossomed
my trees
Unburden in the blast.
The harvest fails grain and
on tree,
Nor comes to me, nor comes to me.
The stream that bears hopes abreast
my
Turns from its
ever my way pregnant tide.
My laden boat, torn from its rest,
Drifts to the other side.
So all hopes
my are set astray,
And drift and drift
away, away.
The lark sings to the
me at morn,
And wings her skyward-soaring
near me
flight ;
But pleasure dies born,
as soon as
The owl takes the night,
up
And night long and doubly dark
seems ;
I miss the lark, I miss the lark.
