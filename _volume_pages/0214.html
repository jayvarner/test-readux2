LONESOME.
MOTHER 's a-visitin' spend
gone to a
month er two,
An', oh, the house is lonesome whose
ez a nest
birds has flew
To other trees to build ag'in the
; rooms seem
jest bare
so
That the echoes like sperrits from the
run
kitchen to the stair.
The shetters flap lazy-like what they
more 'n
used to do,
Sence mother's gone a-visitin' to spend a
month er two.
We 've killed the fattest chicken an' 've
we
cooked her to a turn ;
We 've made the richest but
gravy, I jest don't
give durn
a
Fur nothin' 'at I drink nothin'
er eat, er 'at I
see.
